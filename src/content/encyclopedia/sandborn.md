## Culture

The Sandborn are a desert people whose identity is expressed through detail and restraint. Their attire carries meaning. Clothing functions as a cultural language, communicating identity and allegiance through fabric and craft in an environment where other markers dissolve in the silt. The reality of desert scarcity shapes everything: how they speak, how they negotiate, how they read a room.

## Politics

Sandborn political life operates through the merchant house structure, with men or women holding leadership positions without apparent exception or comment. Their political instinct is direct and transactional. Positions are stated clearly, obstacles identified without theater. The tension between the sandborn and the Somari is a fault line the Empire exploits most casually and understands least.

## Economy

The Sandborn economy runs on route knowledge and resource control. Merchant houses manage resource production and supply infrastructure with an efficiency that treats intermediaries as waste. Water is the desert's ultimate currency, and whoever controls access to it controls everything downstream.

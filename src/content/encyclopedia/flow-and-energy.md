## Imperial Perspective

The Empire approaches flow, which it calls channeling, as a tool to be understood, assigned, and controlled. Every practitioner is evaluated before training and assigned to the discipline most aligned with their essence: scrying, conjuration, illusion, destruction. The underlying mechanic is consistent across all disciplines: energy is drawn in from the world around the practitioner, stored briefly in the body, then released toward a purpose. 

The toll is real and cumulative. Scrying clouds the eyes and eventually milks them over. Combat mages burn fast and die young. The Directorate mitigates this through mirror-bowls, instruments that absorb the cost of scrying so the practitioner's body doesn't have to. The Empire doesn't deny the danger – it manages it, allocates it, and sends the bill to whoever is least valuable. A single spy who can listen through stone is worth more than a hundred men with blades, and the Empire prices its people accordingly.

## Wildkin Perspective

Where the Empire categorizes and contains, the Wildkin relationship to the world's energy is integrative rather than extractive. It flows through them as it flows through the land – the Visareen singing to stars even as their forests burn, the Somari moving through desert terrain with an intimacy no Imperial map can replicate. The Wildkin don't draw energy in and release it; they exist within it.

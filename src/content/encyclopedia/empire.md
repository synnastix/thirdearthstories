## Culture

The Empire projects a mythology of civilization – order imposed where chaos ruled, heroism where others failed, shelter given to the innocent. It maintains this mythology deliberately and mechanically, scrubbing reports of ash and blood until they're clean enough for parades. 

Internally it runs on hierarchy and performance: recruits are broken and remade, costumes are assigned, and the line between scenario and reality is erased by design. The Legion emphasizes a culture that values obedience dressed as discipline, and group punishment as a tool of mutual surveillance. Even its intelligence apparatus, the Information Directorate, operates on the principle that a man who understands the system can manipulate it, which means the Empire itself is built by people who don't believe their own mythology. It is a civilization held together not by shared values but by shared performance of values.


## Politics

Power in the Empire is layered and deliberately obscured. The Emperor is a name, the councilors are faces for public anger, and the real authority belongs to guild masters and coin barons who purchase law and call it governance. The Directorate exists not to serve the people but to serve whoever holds the levers, and those who hold the levers are comfortable letting conflicts fester when instability is more profitable than resolution. 

The Empire’s operations in Thornhold illustrate this perfectly: the Empire doesn't want peace between the Houses, it wants leverage. The Empire's political genius is that it positions itself as arbiter while quietly feeding the fires it claims to be extinguishing. The Empire's neutrality is its most elaborate fiction.


## Economy

The Empire's economy runs on extraction dressed as order. Resources flow upward through a system of merchant Houses operating under Imperial sanction, each House aligned, openly or covertly, with the factions the Empire claims to have pacified. The quarries, the supply lines, the trade routes: all nominally civilian infrastructure, all functionally instruments of Imperial expansion. The labor at the bottom of this system is either coerced or desperate: Legion recruits drawn from slums, Khuldinai slaves in Thornhold, Rootborn women contracted for work no Imperial wants to claim. 

The Empire doesn't need monsters. It has accountants.

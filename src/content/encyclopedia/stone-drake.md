Native apex predator of the Heartlands region. Legion forces are frequently deployed to address stone drake infestations.

## Physical Description

The stone drake is a heavy-bodied quadruped, roughly twice the mass of large bear species. Its most distinctive feature is a set of short wings that fold so flush against the body — covered in the same dense, grey-brown stone-textured scaling as the rest of the hide — that a motionless drake on a rocky slope reads as geology rather than animal. The wings are not built for sustained flight; they generate enough lift for explosive short-range launches from height or from a crouch. A broad dorsal ridge of calcified bone plates runs from the neck to the tail, terminating in outward-angled spurs. The eyes are amber-gold, wide-set, with a vertical slit pupil and a patience in the gaze that observers consistently describe before they describe anything else.

## Wild Behavior

Stone drakes are ambush predators that do not pursue prey at sustained speed. They locate a position above a game trail, river crossing, or open approach, press flat, and wait. The attack is a drop — several hundred pounds of stone-plated animal from elevation, wings deploying on impact to pin the target. They are solitary, territorial, and concentrated in difficult terrain where bare rock provides ideal cover. They will predate on most creatures in their habitat, and have been known to raid unprotected human settlements on occasion.

## Domesticated Behavior & Uses

Cannot be domesticated.
